Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, widely known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

Key Achievements:

  • Played a crucial role in India's civilian space program and military missile development
  • Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology
  • Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)
  • Awarded India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, in 1997
  • Author of several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds"
"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."
- APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His life continues to inspire millions of people, especially the youth of India, to pursue their dreams and contribute to the nation's development.
